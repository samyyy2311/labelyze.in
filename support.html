Frequently Asked Questions

The Labelyze database currently covers packaged food products with barcodes commonly found in India. If a product is not found, it may be a regional or newer product not yet in the database. You can use the in-app Submit Product feature to contribute the product. Submissions are reviewed and typically added within a few days.

Yes. Barcode lookups run against a local database installed with the app. No internet connection is required for scanning or viewing ingredient analysis. Internet access is only required if you choose to submit a missing product.
Open the app and go to Profile. From there you can set or update your dietary conditions, allergen flags, and preferences. Changes take effect immediately on all subsequent scans.
The grade is derived from a 0 to 100 score calculated across ingredient safety flags, nutritional data, additive density, and regulatory status. A (85 to 100) is clean with few concerns. F (below 40) indicates banned ingredients or multiple serious flags. Every deduction is shown with its reason. See the scoring section on the homepage for full details.
Go to Profile, then Data and Privacy, and disable the Share Product Data toggle. Once disabled, no data of any kind is transmitted from your device.
Labelyze cross-references against multiple international regulators. An ingredient may be permitted by FSSAI but banned or restricted by the EU EFSA, US FDA, or other bodies. The flag shows you the gap between Indian and international standards. Every flag includes the regulatory source and the reason for the restriction.
All data is stored locally on your device. To delete it, either clear the app's data from Android Settings, or uninstall the app. There is no server account to delete.
